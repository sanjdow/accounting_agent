# HGB (Handelsgesetzbuch) — Key Accounting Principles

## § 238 HGB — Obligation to keep books
Every merchant is required to keep books of account and to make the
commercial transactions and the position of the assets of the business
apparent in them according to the principles of proper accounting (GoB).

## § 243 HGB — Principles of proper bookkeeping (GoB)
The annual financial statements must be prepared in accordance with the
principles of proper accounting. They must be clear and arranged so that
they give a true and fair view of the assets, financial position and
earnings of the business.

## § 246 HGB — Completeness principle (Vollständigkeitsgrundsatz)
The annual financial statements must include all assets, liabilities,
accruals, expenses and income unless otherwise provided by law. Offsetting
of assets against liabilities, or of expenses against income, is
generally prohibited.

## § 250 HGB — Prepaid expenses and deferred income (Rechnungsabgrenzung)
Expenditures made before the balance sheet date which represent expense
for a specific period after that date must be reported on the asset
side as prepaid expenses. Likewise, receipts before the balance sheet
date representing income for a specific period after that date must be
reported on the liability side as deferred income.

## § 252 HGB — Recognition and measurement principles
(1) The following principles must be observed:
   1. Balance sheet continuity — opening balances must equal prior closing balances.
   2. Going concern principle — valuation is based on the continuation of the business.
   3. Individual valuation — assets and liabilities are valued individually.
   4. Prudence principle (Vorsichtsprinzip): all foreseeable risks and
      losses that arose before the balance sheet date must be taken into
      account, even if they only became known between the balance sheet
      date and the date of preparation; profits are recognised only
      when realised (Realisationsprinzip).
   5. Accrual principle — income and expenses of the financial year are
      recognised in that period regardless of payment timing
      (Periodenabgrenzung).
   6. Consistency principle — valuation methods applied in the preceding
      annual financial statements must be retained.

## § 253 HGB — Measurement
Assets must be valued at acquisition or production cost less depreciation.
Provisions for uncertain liabilities and provisions for impending losses
from pending transactions must be recognised at the settlement amount
required by prudent commercial judgement.

## § 255 HGB — Acquisition and production cost
Acquisition cost comprises the expenses incurred to acquire an asset
and bring it to an operational condition, to the extent that they can be
assigned to the asset individually.

## § 266 HGB — Balance sheet structure
The balance sheet must be presented in account form, with assets on the
left and equity/liabilities on the right. Current and non-current items
must be separately disclosed.

## Month-end close checklist (GoB-compliant)
- Verify opening balances equal prior period closing balances.
- Ensure all period accruals (RAP) are booked in the correct period.
- Reconcile GL to subsidiary ledgers (AR, AP, fixed assets).
- Review impairment indicators for long-lived assets.
- Disclose material events after the balance sheet date
  (wertaufhellend vs. wertbegründend).
- Obtain management approval before sealing the period.
