# IFRS — Key Standards Relevant to Month-End Close

## IAS 1 — Presentation of Financial Statements
Financial statements must present fairly the financial position,
financial performance and cash flows of an entity. A complete set of
financial statements comprises a statement of financial position,
statement of profit or loss and other comprehensive income, statement of
changes in equity, statement of cash flows, and accompanying notes.
Going concern, accrual basis of accounting, materiality and aggregation,
offsetting, and frequency of reporting are core presentation principles.

## IAS 8 — Accounting Policies, Changes in Accounting Estimates and Errors
Accounting policies must be applied consistently for similar transactions.
A change in accounting policy is permitted only if required by an IFRS
or if it results in reliable and more relevant information. Prior period
errors are corrected retrospectively by restating comparative amounts.

## IAS 10 — Events after the Reporting Period
Adjusting events (providing evidence of conditions that existed at the
end of the reporting period) require adjustment to amounts recognised.
Non-adjusting events (indicative of conditions that arose after the
reporting period) are disclosed if material. Dividends declared after
the reporting period are not a liability at the end of the period.

## IAS 16 — Property, Plant and Equipment
PPE is initially measured at cost and subsequently at cost less
accumulated depreciation and impairment, or at revalued amount.
Depreciation begins when the asset is available for use and continues
until the asset is derecognised.

## IAS 36 — Impairment of Assets
At the end of each reporting period an entity assesses whether there is
any indication that an asset may be impaired. If any such indication
exists, the entity estimates the recoverable amount of the asset.
An impairment loss is recognised immediately in profit or loss unless
the asset is carried at revalued amount. Indicators of impairment
include significant decline in market value, adverse changes in
technology, significant underperformance relative to historical or
projected results.

## IAS 37 — Provisions, Contingent Liabilities and Contingent Assets
A provision is recognised when an entity has a present obligation
(legal or constructive) as a result of a past event, it is probable
that an outflow of resources will be required, and a reliable estimate
can be made. Provisions are measured at the best estimate of the
expenditure required to settle the obligation at the reporting date.

## IFRS 15 — Revenue from Contracts with Customers
Revenue is recognised when control of goods or services transfers to
the customer, applying a 5-step model:
  1. Identify the contract with the customer.
  2. Identify the performance obligations.
  3. Determine the transaction price.
  4. Allocate the transaction price to the performance obligations.
  5. Recognise revenue when (or as) performance obligations are satisfied.

## IFRS 16 — Leases
A lessee recognises a right-of-use asset and a lease liability for all
leases, except short-term leases and leases of low-value assets.
The lease liability is initially measured at the present value of the
lease payments not yet paid.

## IFRS 10 — Consolidated Financial Statements
An investor controls an investee when it is exposed, or has rights,
to variable returns from its involvement with the investee and has the
ability to affect those returns through its power over the investee.
The financial statements of the parent and its subsidiaries used in
preparing the consolidated financial statements shall be prepared as
of the same reporting date.

## Month-end close checklist (IFRS)
- Confirm reporting dates align across parent and subsidiaries (IFRS 10).
- Review all accruals and provisions for completeness (IAS 37).
- Perform impairment indicator assessment for material assets (IAS 36).
- Ensure revenue recognition follows the 5-step model (IFRS 15).
- Update right-of-use asset and lease liability carrying amounts (IFRS 16).
- Document significant judgements and estimation uncertainties (IAS 1).
- Identify and classify events after the reporting period (IAS 10).
